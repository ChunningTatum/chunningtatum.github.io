Mohi

In this challenge I will be focussing on the challenge: “Information structure: How can we improve the user experience of the information architecture of all the different elements of the application.” In this challenge I will be brainstorming about the IA of the app, how the userflow, if the labelling is correct, and testing & interviewing users to see if the app works as is intended. How can we improve the information architecture of the app Mohi? Right now the app is difficult to use for the users that have been tested. I want to design an infrastructure that is intuitive for the user, so the user spends less time looking around, searching for features.

What is Mohi?

Today’s social media is full of noise and distractions that actually lead to more isolated and unhappy lives. With Mohi we want to build the world’s first ‘Slow Media’ platform where people can organize their digital lives and strengthen relationships by sharing their favorite content experiences. On this platform, users are able to save some of their favorite content (articles, books, podcasts, movies, series, documentaries, songs) and share their selection of highlights with their inner circle. In this way, Mohi becomes the app where you can always find the best content recommendations from people you trust.

Mohi is all about:

  • De-clutter your digital life. Start collecting and organizing all your favorite content discoveries (articles, movies, podcasts, books, documentaries, songs)
  • Get recommendations from people you trust. Discover the monthly selection of content recommendations from your inner circle.
  • Discover tips from selected curators. We’ll constantly update the app with tips from your favourite journalists, artists, poets, movie producers, entrepreneurs, and other wicked souls.

First look at Mohi

By casually browsing the Mohi app and using it, I have noticed that a lot of the functionality did not work as intended. The app was confusing to use and most of the time the app did not do things I thought it would. Understanding this I decided to redesign the information architecture of the app. The user shouldn’t press every button there is to find a certain functionality so that’s why I have decided to improve the IA structure.

Insights after use:

  • What or who are curators?
  • Why do only curators have a ‘Bio’?
  • Why can’t I look at other people’s top 5?
  • The UI is kind of confusing and not suitable for users with a vision impairment.
  • Why does Saved for later have its own tab in the navigation?
  • It is kind off hard to search for certain functionality because the labels are not clear to me.

User research

Before improving the IA structure, we need to know how users use the app and we decided to look for people to interview. In the interview we will be asking beta users that have been using the app and other people that might be interested in the app.

In our interview, people mostly use Instagram and YouTube, thus I will be analysing these social media apps. I will also be analysing Facebook, because the target audience will be people at the age of 30 – 40 years. Based on interviews and AI structure analysis of YouTube, Instagram and Facebook, I came up with this structure.

Insights from the interviews:

  • Users do not understand what Mohi means.
  • Users do not understand what Curators mean.
  • It takes a lot of time for the users to navigate to certain functionalities, they are difficult to find.
  • There are a lot of things that don’t work the way it was intended.
  • The saved for later button on the navigation should move to a different place.
  • Labels should have a better desicription.
  • There was a suggestion to see how Instagram’s IA structure works.
  • There should be a better way to show users what kind of content they can share.

Validating concept

To verify my designs, I planned in new interviews and testing. In these tests I will be testing two different kind of users. The first group of testers are the people I have interviewed before, these users already have used the beta app and already know how the app works. The first group of testers are to test that the new design is a improvement. The second group of testers are in the target group of the app and have never used the beta before, so they will be going into the app blind without prior knowledge.

Test insights:

  • Beta users clear almost all the test under 2 minutes
  • The Highlight of the month part might need to be placed elsewhere
  • The add content button does work and it shows from user research, but from design critique and feedback from teachers, it is discussable if it is the best place to be placed in.
  • All the users I have tested are able to understand and find the functions that the Mohi has. These tasks are also done in times faster than the app right now.
