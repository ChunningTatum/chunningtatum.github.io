Lockdown air pollution

The KNMI approached us to develop an interactive and insightful tool for politicians and the general public to understand the impact of collective behavior on air pollution. With the reduction in car and air traffic during the early stages of the Covid-19 pandemic, the air became significantly cleaner. Utilizing data from the Dutch TROPOMI satellite, which has been measuring air pollution globally since 2018, we were able to analyze the effects of the lockdown on various cities. From our provisional analysis, we found that cities in South-Europe and Paris were 60% cleaner compared to pre-pandemic levels. However, it is important to note that not all cities had the same level of reduction, as measurements varied in different areas. Our goal is to create a user-friendly tool that effectively communicates these findings and educates users on the impact of their actions on air pollution.

How do we visualise data for policy makers and the general public?

Our initial idea is to collect data regarding Covid-19 infections and measurements during a certain time frame. After getting enough data and information, we started brainstorming and sketching different ideas. The table to the right is a collection of all the data we have collected. The developer made a small slider for us to decide the country's we would measure against each other.

Concept iteration 1

For our first concept, we wanted to paint a story for the user. First we would introduce background information about NO2 and we would ask the user some questions. The user would then choose a country, where we would then give them some data regarding Covid-19 in the country, the average NO2 emissions in the chosen country and a lot more of the data sets we have found.

Feedback iteration 1

  • The concept is not aimed at the right target group.
  • Comparing cities is a nice idea, but don't make it too big.
  • What value do you want to give to the user when using your product?

Concept iteration 2

Based on the feedback we had received, we mainly focused on comparing the two cities from different continents. We decided to put off the narrative part for now and started looking for a style in which we wanted to design our concept. In iteration 2, we shifted the focus from policy makers and the general public to policy makers only. the client stated that the user is mainly interested in finding connections in data, and this is also something a policy maker would like to do. By focussing on only one target group, our focus was clear. On the right is a image off different versions of the graph.

Feedback iteration 2

  • show exactly where the measures have been taken.
  • How do you show the comparison between Covid-19 measures?
  • Give the user some explanation because the interaction is unique (but very cool).

Concept iteration 3

In this iteration we have added a tutorial page that explains how our comparison tool works. We have done this because our graph is very complex and we can better guide the user with it. We also intergrated the storytelling part of the concept. For the live website, click the link at the end!

Feedback iteration 3

  • Change weeks to months on the X axis.
  • Create some zero-states'.
  • Guide the user in data that are suspicious.
