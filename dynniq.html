Re-design the 'OPCIS' product in a more modern look and feel that focusses on mobile and user interaction first.

Dynniq Energy focuses on improving energy supplies. This includes the distribution of cables and pipelines to neighborhoods and households, as well as the engineering and construction of high-voltage network stations. The company is a recognized player in enhancing the electricity grid to achieve a low-carbon energy economy. Major customers include network operators such as Liander, Stedin, and TenneT. Since 2019, Dynniq Energy B.V. has been operating independently in the Dutch energy market.

About OPCIS

OPCIS is a business management system that can communicate with DSP, where all the energy grid operators communicate with. As an example, when OPCIS receives a task from Liander, it generates a work order, which is then assigned to a mechanic. Since the application is built on an old framework (Java JSF v8), it was more efficient to address the mechanic flow first, ensuring that changes wouldn't impact other components of OPCIS. My role at the OPCIS team was to prepare and implement the redesign for the Heijmans acquisition and simultaneously improve the platform's user experience and provide support as a DevOps engineer.

Mobile first

The first big project in the team, is to improve the UI for the mechanics that interact with the system on a mobile/tablet. The redesign increased the ease of use of the application by a large margin. Elements were also designed in a way that was more accessible to people with larger hands.

Information Architecture

To properly understand the interaction between mechanic and system, it is important to go through the journey of the system and where the pain points are. In the project Heijmans I will go more in depth about design decisions in the flow. For now we will be focusing on the redesign. Because the interfaces will mostly be used on a small interface, I did not put a lot of effort into the large screen version. So I won't be covering that part.
